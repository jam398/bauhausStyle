## Historical Bauhaus References

### Original Bauhaus Works to Study

#### 1. **Bauhaus Posters (1920s-1930s)**

**Key Works:**
- Joost Schmidt - Bauhaus Exhibition poster (1923)
- Herbert Bayer - Bauhaus Dessau poster (1926)
- László Moholy-Nagy - Photomontage posters
- Oskar Schlemmer - Triadic Ballet posters

**Where to Find:**
- Google Arts & Culture: "Bauhaus posters"
- MoMA Collection Online
- V&A Museum Collection
- Bauhaus Archive Berlin (online collection)

**Search Terms:**
- "Bauhaus poster 1920s"
- "Herbert Bayer poster"
- "Bauhaus exhibition poster"
- "Bauhaus typography"

#### 2. **Typography & Graphic Design**

**Key Works:**
- Herbert Bayer - Universal typeface (1925)
- Joost Schmidt - Geometric typography experiments
- Bauhaus magazines (covers and layouts)
- László Moholy-Nagy - Photo-typography

**Where to Find:**
- Fonts In Use: bauhausfontsinuse
- Type Archive online
- Museum of Design Zurich

**Search Terms:**
- "Bauhaus universal typeface"
- "Bauhaus typography geometric"
- "Herbert Bayer font design"

**Search Terms:**
- "Bauhaus Dessau building"

## Design Books & Publications

### Essential Bauhaus Books (Physical/PDF)

1. **"Grid Systems in Graphic Design"** - Josef Müller-Brockmann (1961)
   - THE grid system bible
   - Available: Amazon, book depository, PDFs online

2. **"Bauhaus 1919-1933"** - Magdalena Droste (Taschen, 2019)
   - Comprehensive visual history
   - High-quality reproductions
   - Available: Bookstores, Amazon

3. **"The Bauhaus"** - Frank Whitford (Thames & Hudson, 1984)
   - Accessible introduction
   - Good visuals

4. **"Bauhaus: Workshops for Modernity"** - Barry Bergdoll (MoMA, 2009)
   - Exhibition catalog
   - Excellent workshop examples

5. **"Typography: A Manual of Design"** - Emil Ruder (1967)
   - Swiss typography
   - Grid-based layouts
   - Hard to find, search for PDFs

### Contemporary Design Books

1. **"Designing with Web Standards"** - Jeffrey Zeldman (2003)
   - Web standards movement
   - Semantic HTML

2. **"Don't Make Me Think"** - Steve Krug (2000)
   - Usability, simplicity
   - Aligns with Bauhaus "form follows function"

3. **"The Elements of Typographic Style"** - Robert Bringhurst (1992)
   - Typography principles
   - Applies to web

## How to Save & Organize References

### Folder Structure

```
visual-references/
├── 01-historical-bauhaus/
│   ├── posters/
│   ├── typography/
│   ├── architecture/
│   └── furniture/
├── 02-contemporary-websites/
│   ├── linear/
│   ├── stripe/
│   ├── vercel/
│   └── figma/
├── 03-typography-examples/
├── 04-color-palettes/
├── 05-grid-systems/
└── 06-inspiration/
```

### Naming Convention

**Format:** `source-subject-descriptor.jpg`

**Examples:**
- `linear-homepage-hero.jpg`
- `bauhaus-poster-1926-bayer.jpg`
- `stripe-pricing-table.jpg`
- `moma-wassily-chair-breuer.jpg`

### Metadata to Record

Create a `references.md` file:

```markdown
# Visual References Log

## Linear.app Homepage
- **URL:** https://linear.app
- **Date Captured:** 2025-11-06
- **Key Features:** 
  - Inter font
  - Blue/purple gradient accent
  - Grid-based layout
  - Clean navigation
- **Why Relevant:** Modern neo-modernist, performance-focused
- **Files:** 
  - linear-homepage-hero.jpg
  - linear-features-grid.jpg
  - linear-pricing.jpg
```

### Tools for Capturing

1. **Screenshot (Native)**
   - Windows: Win + Shift + S
   - Mac: Cmd + Shift + 4
   - Crop to relevant area

2. **Full Page Screenshots**
   - Chrome extension: "Full Page Screen Capture"
   - Firefox: Built-in (Shift + F2, type "screenshot --fullpage")

3. **Design Tool Exports**
   - Figma: Export frames as PNG/JPG
   - Sketch: Export artboards

4. **Pinterest (Optional)**
   - Create private board
   - Pin references with notes
   - Good for mood boards

## Key Takeaways

### What Makes a Good Reference?

1. **Authentic** - Real Bauhaus or true neo-modernist work
2. **Clear** - High resolution, good screenshot
3. **Documented** - Source, date, designer known
4. **Relevant** - Applies to your project (web design)
5. **Varied** - Mix of historical and contemporary

### Reference Usage Guidelines

- **Inspiration, not copying** - Understand principles, don't clone
- **Multiple sources** - Synthesize from 5+ references
- **Adapt** - Make it your own interpretation
- **Credit** - Acknowledge influences in documentation
